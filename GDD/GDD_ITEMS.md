# GDD\_ITEMS

## Overview

Items are tactical tools.
Items support board interaction and player conflict.
Items are not the primary progression system.

Primary progression systems:

* Trophies
* Buildings
* Dice

## Categories

### Utility Items

Strategic items focused on:

* Mobility
* Economy
* Planning
* Board interaction

### Warfare Items

Aggressive items focused on:

* Sabotage
* Theft
* Combat
* Disruption

## Inventory

8 Item Slots

## Rarity System

* Common — frequent in shop, cheapest
* Uncommon — less frequent, more expensive
* Rare — rare in shop, expensive
* Legendary — very rare, most expensive

Rarity affects both shop appearance chance and price.
Exact prices and appearance rates to be determined during playtesting.

## Shop Rules

Randomized per player.

Guaranteed:

* Minimum 1 Utility Item
* Minimum 1 Warfare Item

## Item Acquisition

* Shop
* Production Building rewards
* Board rewards
* Events

## Utility Items

### Extra Roll

During dice roll phase, roll twice and choose which result to use.
Rarity: Common

### Shield

Ignore the next negative effect targeting you.
Blocks: Warfare Items, some Events, some Control Building effects.
Rarity: Common

### Health Kit

Restore full HP.
Rarity: Common

### Copy Cat

Choose one option:

* Use the previous player's dice result as your own movement.
* Force a chosen player to reroll and use their new result as your own movement.
Rarity: Uncommon

### Reverse

Move backwards on the board this turn.
Rarity: Uncommon

### Loaded Dice

Choose your exact movement result instead of rolling dice.
Does not affect other dice effects (e.g. Jackpot).
Rarity: Rare

### Room Key

Unlocks entry to the Special Room.
Does not teleport — you must still travel there.
Casino variant: VIP Key
Rarity: Rare

### Remote Shop

Purchase an item from anywhere on the board without visiting a shop tile.
Rarity: Rare

### Swap

Switch board positions with a chosen player.
Rarity: Legendary

## Warfare Items

### Trap

Place a trap on a chosen tile.
The first player who lands on it loses Coins or receives a debuff.
Rarity: Common

### Punch

Deal damage to a chosen player in range.
Target is pushed back several tiles.
Range and tile count to be determined during playtesting.
Rarity: Uncommon

### Rocket

Launch a rocket at any player regardless of distance.
Target loses Coins and receives damage.
Rarity: Uncommon

### Magnet

Pull a random amount of Coins from a chosen player in range.
Small chance to also steal a random item.
Range to be determined during playtesting.
Rarity: Uncommon

### Bomb

Assign a bomb to a chosen player.
After X turns the bomb explodes — target loses Coins and receives damage.
The target can pass the bomb to another player by making contact with them.
Turn count to be determined during playtesting.
Rarity: Uncommon

### Cursed Dice

Choose which die from the target's collection they must use next turn.
Target rolls that die twice and must use the worse result.
Rarity: Rare

### Lucky Bastard

Until the start of your next turn, the first negative effect aimed at you is redirected to a random opponent.
Rarity: Legendary

### Loaded Investigation

View a chosen player's full item inventory and dice collection.
Then choose one item or one die — steal it or destroy it.
Rarity: Legendary

## Canon Decisions

* Items are tactical tools, not primary progression.
* Dice and Items are separate systems.
* Dice do not occupy item slots.
* Items have four rarity tiers: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Legendary.
* Rarity affects shop frequency and price.
* Shop is randomized per player.
* Shop guarantees minimum 1 Utility and 1 Warfare Item.
