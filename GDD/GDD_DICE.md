# GDD_DICE

## Overview
Dice are a separate progression category — they are not Items and have no rarity tiers.
Players select a Dice before each movement phase.
Dice are reusable and permanent for the duration of a match.

## Dice Collection
- Unlimited collection size
- No Dice Slots
- No Dice Loadouts
- Player selects one Dice before each movement

Dice are intended to be rare and situational.
Players are expected to own 2-3 Dice and choose based on their current needs.
Examples: use a precise Dice to land on a specific tile, use a travel Dice to cover ground quickly.

## Dice Acquisition
- Shop
- Production Building rewards
- Board rewards
- Casino rewards
- Events

## Accepted Dice

### Basic Dice
Faces: 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8
Standard movement with average range.

### Safe Dice
Faces: 3 3 4 4 5 5 6 6
Removes low and high variance — reliable mid-range movement.

### Travel Dice
Faces: 2 2 4 4 8 8 10 10
High variance with strong high-end results — good for covering distance quickly.

### Risk Dice
Faces: 0 0 0 0 10 10 10 10
All-or-nothing — either no movement or maximum movement.

### Jackpot Dice
Faces: 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 Jackpot Bust
A high-risk entertainment Dice. Rolling Jackpot or Bust replaces normal movement with a special outcome.

## Jackpot Outcome Philosophy
Most Jackpot outcomes are small rewards.
Medium rewards are uncommon.
Major rewards are very rare.

Examples:
- +1 to +5 Coins
- +1 to +5 Movement
- Rare: Extra Roll
- Very Rare: VIP Key

Teleport effects are intentionally excluded from Jackpot rewards.

## Bust Outcome Philosophy
Bust should feel dangerous but not game-ending.
Bust outcomes are small setbacks.

Examples:
- Coin loss
- Negative movement
- Item loss

## Canon Decisions
- Dice are a separate category from Items.
- Dice have no rarity tiers.
- Dice collection is unlimited.
- No Dice Slots or Loadouts.
- Dice are reusable.
- Jackpot Dice excludes teleport effects.
